# Calculation of Young's Modulus (Graphing Method)

> [!abstract] Method Description
> According to the experimental requirements, the Young's Modulus $Y$ should be determined by the **slope** of the Stress-Strain curve, rather than calculating the average of individual measurements.
> 
> **Steps:**
> 1. Plot **Stress ($\sigma$)** on the y-axis and **Strain ($\varepsilon$)** on the x-axis.
> 2. Draw the best-fit straight line passing through the data points.
> 3. Select two distinct points on the fitted line, $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$, which are far apart to minimize relative error.
> 4. Calculate the slope $k$ using the formula: $k = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$.

---

## 1. Selection of Points on the Fitted Line

Based on the linear fit of the experimental data, we select two points on the line to calculate the slope.

Point A (Lower region):
  Strain ($\varepsilon_1$) = $2.00 \times 10^{-4}$
  Corresponding Stress ($\sigma_1$) $\approx 4.06 \times 10^7 \text{ Pa}$ 

Point B (Upper region):
  Strain ($\varepsilon_2$) = $1.20 \times 10^{-3}$
  Corresponding Stress ($\sigma_2$) $\approx 2.436 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}$ 

---

## 2. Calculation Process

The Young's Modulus $Y$ is numerically equal to the slope $k$ of the $\sigma - \varepsilon$ graph.

$$
\begin{aligned}
Y = \text{Slope} &= \frac{\Delta \sigma}{\Delta \varepsilon} \\
&= \frac{\sigma_2 - \sigma_1}{\varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_1} \\
&= \frac{(2.436 \times 10^8 - 0.406 \times 10^8) \, \text{Pa}}{(1.20 \times 10^{-3} - 0.20 \times 10^{-3})} \\
&= \frac{2.030 \times 10^8 \, \text{Pa}}{1.00 \times 10^{-3}} \\
&= 2.03 \times 10^{11} \, \text{Pa}
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## 3. Final Result

The Young's Modulus of the steel wire, determined from the graph, is:

$$\mathbf{Y = 2.03 \times 10^{11} \, \text{Pa}}$$

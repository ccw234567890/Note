# 2.3 Calculation of Young's Modulus

## 1. Selection of Points on the Fitted Line

Based on the linear fit of the experimental data ($g=10 \, m/s^2$), we select two points on the line to calculate the slope.

Point A (Lower region):
 Strain ($\varepsilon_1$) = $0.20 \times 10^{-3}$
 Corresponding Stress ($\sigma_1$) $\approx 0.424 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}$

Point B (Upper region):
Strain ($\varepsilon_2$) = $1.20 \times 10^{-3}$
Corresponding Stress ($\sigma_2$) $\approx 2.494 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}$

---

## 2. Calculation Process

The Young's Modulus $Y$ is numerically equal to the slope $k$ of the $\sigma - \varepsilon$ graph.

$$
\begin{aligned}
Y = \text{Slope} &= \frac{\Delta \sigma}{\Delta \varepsilon} \\
&= \frac{\sigma_2 - \sigma_1}{\varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_1} \\
&= \frac{(2.494 \times 10^8 - 0.424 \times 10^8) \, \text{Pa}}{(1.20 \times 10^{-3} - 0.20 \times 10^{-3})} \\
&= \frac{2.070 \times 10^8 \, \text{Pa}}{1.00 \times 10^{-3}} \\
&= 2.07 \times 10^{11} \, \text{Pa}
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## 3. Final Result

The Young's Modulus of the steel wire, determined from the graph, is:

$$
\mathbf{Y = 2.07 \times 10^{11} \, \text{Pa}}
$$

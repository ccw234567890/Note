## 1. Abstract
*(Updated for $g=10 m/s^2$)*

This experiment aims to measure the Young's Modulus of a steel wire using the method of elongation. The microscopic elongation of the wire under tensile stress was magnified and measured using an optical lever combined with a telescope and scale system. The geometric parameters of the system were measured: the original length $L = 644.0 \text{ mm}$, diameter $d = 0.578 \text{ mm}$, mirror-to-scale distance $D = 1532.0 \text{ mm}$, and optical lever arm $b = 68.88 \text{ mm}$. The uncertainty in the diameter was calculated to be 0.004 mm. By plotting the Stress vs. Strain curve (using $g=10 \, m/s^2$), the Young's Modulus was determined from the slope to be approximately **$2.07 \times 10^{11} \text{ Pa}$**. The results verify Hooke's Law within the elastic limit.

---

## 2. Data Processing

### 2.1 Uncertainty in Steel Diameter $d$ 

**Answer:**

1.A-Type Uncertainty ($u_A$):

Based on the 6 measurements from Table 1: $0.575, 0.585, 0.581, 0.580, 0.569, 0.575$ (mm).
*Mean ($\bar{d}$):
 $$\bar{d} = \frac{\sum d_i}{6} = 0.5775 \, \text{mm}$$
 Standard Deviation ($S_d$):
$$S_d = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (d_i - \bar{d})^2}{n-1}} \approx 0.00565 \, \text{mm}$$
Standard Deviation of the Mean ($u_A$):
$$u_A = \frac{S_d}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{0.00565}{\sqrt{6}} \approx \mathbf{0.00231 \, \text{mm}}$$

2.B-Type Uncertainty ($u_B$):
Instrument error ($\Delta_{inst}$) = 0.005 mm; Reading error ($\Delta_{read}$) = 0.002 mm.
Assuming uniform distribution ($k=\sqrt{3}$):
$$u_B = \sqrt{\frac{\Delta_{inst}^2 + \Delta_{read}^2}{3}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.005^2 + 0.002^2}{3}} \approx \mathbf{0.00311 \, \text{mm}}$$

3.Combined Uncertainty ($U_d$):
$$U_d = \sqrt{u_A^2 + u_B^2} = \sqrt{0.00231^2 + 0.00311^2} \approx 0.00387 \, \text{mm}$$

**Result:**
$$U_d \approx \mathbf{0.004 \, \text{mm}}$$

---

### 2.2 Calculation of Young's Modulus (Graphing Method)
*(Recalculated with $g=10 m/s^2$)*

**1. Data Points Adjustment:**
Since $g$ changed from 9.79 to 10 (an increase of $\approx 2.1\%$), all Stress values increase proportionally.
* Example (Strain $\varepsilon = 1.20 \times 10^{-3}$):
    * Old Stress ($g=9.79$) $\approx 2.436 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}$
    * New Stress ($g=10$) $= 2.436 \times \frac{10}{9.79} \approx \mathbf{2.488 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}}$

**2. Calculation from Slope:**
We select two points on the fitted line:
* **Point A:** $\varepsilon_1 = 0.20 \times 10^{-3}$, $\sigma_1 \approx 0.415 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}$
* **Point B:** $\varepsilon_2 = 1.20 \times 10^{-3}$, $\sigma_2 \approx 2.488 \times 10^8 \text{ Pa}$

$$
Y = \frac{\sigma_2 - \sigma_1}{\varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_1} = \frac{2.073 \times 10^8 \, \text{Pa}}{1.00 \times 10^{-3}} = \mathbf{2.07 \times 10^{11} \, \text{Pa}}
$$

---

## 3. Conclusion
*(Summary and Error Analysis)*

In this experiment, we successfully determined the elastic modulus of the steel specimen.
1.  **Results:** Using the gravitational acceleration **$g=10 \, m/s^2$**, the Young's Modulus was calculated to be **$2.07 \times 10^{11} \text{ Pa}$**. This value is slightly higher than the standard theoretical value ($2.0 \times 10^{11} \text{ Pa}$) but remains reasonable within experimental error.
2.  **Linearity:** The Stress-Strain relationship proved to be linear, verifying Hooke's Law.
3.  **Error Analysis:**
    * **Systematic Error:** The uncertainty in diameter $d$ ($U_d \approx 0.004 \text{ mm}$) is the dominant error source.
    * **Random Error:** Visual estimation on the scale reading caused minor fluctuations.

### Q3.1 Reading & Instrument Error (4 points)
**Question:** What is the reading shown in Fig. 1? What is the instrument error?
**Answer:**
* **Reading:** $3.5 \text{ mm} \text{ (Main)} + 0.385 \text{ mm} \text{ (Thimble)} = \mathbf{3.885 \text{ mm}}$.
* **Instrument Error:** Typically **0.004 mm** or **0.005 mm** for a 0.01mm micrometer.

### Q3.2 Proper Placement of Optical Lever (6 points)
**Question:** How can the optical lever be placed properly on the flat?
**Answer:**
1.  **Front Toes:** Must be placed into the **transverse grooves** on the platform.
2.  **Back Toe:** Must be placed on the **surface of the clamp** holding the wire.
3.  **Precautions:** Do not touch the wire; ensure the back toe is not in the cracks.

### Q3.3 Reasons for Not Observing the Meter Stick (10 points)
**Question:** Analyze reasons why the meter stick cannot be observed.
**Answer:**
1.  **Horizontal Misalignment:** Telescope not pointing at the mirror center.
2.  **Vertical Height:** Telescope height not matching the optical lever.
3.  **Mirror Tilt:** Mirror tilted too high (ceiling) or too low (floor).
4.  **Focus:** Telescope not focused on the **reflected image** (scale), or image distance incorrect.
5.  **Obstruction:** Light path blocked by equipment.
